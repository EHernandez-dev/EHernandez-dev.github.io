Speech Recognition for Non-Normative Speech

Making automatic speech recognition work for every voice

The problem

Automatic speech recognition has become remarkably good, as long as you speak the way the training data does. For people whose speech falls outside that norm, for example due to speech or motor impairments such as dysarthria, standard ASR models often fail entirely. The very technology that could ease everyday communication ends up excluding the people who would benefit from it most.

The project

In this project at the appliedAI Institute for Europe, we develop AI solutions for non-standard speech data. Instead of assuming one "standard language", the models are adapted to the individual: they learn a person's own patterns of expression and transcribe their spoken language in real time.

The approach was inspired by a colleague with spastic dysarthria, for whom an AI model trained on his specific speech patterns opened up day-to-day communication at work. What made the difference was not a bigger general-purpose model, but individualized training on the speech of one person.

Toward barrier-free education

Together with the AI in Education Competence Center of the aim academy in Heilbronn, we work on carrying this approach into educational practice. If spoken language can be transcribed reliably for every voice, communication in classrooms becomes more barrier-free, and educational participation becomes more equitable for learners with speech impairments.

You can read more in this interview (in German) with Dr. Wintai Tsehaye of the aim academy, where the project is discussed: "Wie KI sprachliche Teilhabe in der Bildung verbessern kann".
